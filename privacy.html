Facet Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026

Facet ("Facet", "we", "us") is a mobile app that finds the people in your photos and delivers each person the pictures they're in. Because doing that involves facial recognition and your photos, we take privacy seriously and explain here exactly what we collect, why, who we share it with, and the controls you have.

This policy applies to the Facet mobile app and related services. By creating an account and using Facet, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use Facet.

The short version. Facet scans your camera roll to recognise faces, groups your photos into albums by the people and places in them, and lets you share those albums with the people who appear in them. Face recognition uses your explicit consent. You can delete your face data, your content, and your entire account at any time from within the app.

1. Information we collect

Account information

  • Email address — to create and secure your account and to send you invites/notifications.
  • Phone number (optional) — if you choose to add it, to help friends find or connect with you.
  • Display name and profile photo — shown to people you share albums with.

Photos and content

  • Camera-roll photos — with your permission, Facet reads photos from your device to detect faces and group them into moments. Analysis of your library happens on your device and via our secure processing services (see below).
  • Shared album photos — when a photo is placed into an album that is shared with someone, that photo is uploaded to our secure storage so the recipient can view it. Photos that are not part of a shared album are not uploaded to our servers as part of album delivery.
  • Reactions and comments — content you add to albums or photos.

Biometric / face data

To recognise people, Facet creates facial-geometry data ("face templates") from the faces in your photos and from the selfies you register. This is biometric information. We collect and use it only with your explicit consent, which we ask for during onboarding before any face is registered. See Section 4 for full detail.

Location information

  • Photo location (EXIF/GPS) — where your photos contain embedded location data, we read it to group your moments by place (for example, "Porto" or "Lisboa") and to place them on your map.
  • Device location permission — used only to improve place labelling. We do not track your live location in the background.

Contacts

If you choose to tag someone or invite them, we use the name and contact details (such as email or phone) that you provide for that person so we can connect their photos to them or send them an invite. We do not upload your entire address book without your action.

Device and usage information

  • Push notification token — to send you notifications about new albums and activity.
  • Basic technical/diagnostic data — such as app version and error logs, to keep the app working.

2. How we use your information

  • Detect and recognise the people in your photos and group them into albums (with your consent for face recognition).
  • Deliver each person the album of photos they appear in, when you share it.
  • Organise your memories by person, place, and time.
  • Send you notifications about new albums, reactions, comments, and invites.
  • Operate, secure, debug, and improve the service.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

3. How we share your information

With people you share with

When you share an album, the people you share it with can see the photos in that album, your name and profile photo, and any reactions or comments. Facet's purpose is to deliver people the photos they're in — sharing only happens as a result of your actions (creating/sharing albums, tagging, inviting).

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use your photos or face data for advertising.

4. Face recognition and biometric data

  • Consent. We create and use face templates only after you give explicit consent during onboarding. You can decline; face recognition simply won't run.
  • Purpose. Face templates are used solely to recognise the same person across your photos so their album can be assembled and delivered. They are not used for any other purpose.
  • Processing. Face detection and matching are performed via AWS Rekognition on our behalf. Face templates are stored in a secure, access-controlled collection.
  • Retention. We keep your face templates only as long as your account is active and you have face recognition enabled. When you re-register your face, your previous templates are replaced. When you delete your face data or your account, the associated templates are deleted (see Section 6).
  • No sale or ad use. We never sell biometric data or use it for advertising or profiling.

5. Legal bases (for users in the EU/UK)

Where the GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we rely on: your consent (for face recognition and photo access), performance of a contract (to provide the service you signed up for), and our legitimate interests (to secure and improve the app). You can withdraw consent at any time.

6. Your rights and choices

  • Access & portability — you can request a copy of your data.
  • Correction — you can update your profile in the app.
  • Deletion — you can delete your face data, individual albums, and your entire account from the app's settings. Deleting your account removes your account data, your uploaded shared photos, and your face templates from our systems.
  • Withdraw consent — you can turn off/withdraw face recognition consent and delete your face data at any time.
  • Permissions — you can change photo, contacts, location, and notification permissions in your device settings at any time.

Depending on where you live (for example, the EU/UK under GDPR, or California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have additional rights. To exercise any right, use the in-app controls or contact us (Section 11).

7. Data retention

We keep your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the service. When you delete content, an album, your face data, or your account, we delete the corresponding data from our active systems; residual copies in encrypted backups are removed on our regular backup rotation.

8. Security

We protect your data with access controls and row-level security so that users can only access their own data and the content shared with them. Sensitive fields (such as email, phone, and notification tokens) are restricted so they are not readable by other users. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information and to fix issues promptly.

9. International data transfers

Facet uses providers that may process data in regions including the European Union and the United States. Where data is transferred internationally, we rely on our providers' safeguards and appropriate legal transfer mechanisms.

10. Children's privacy

Facet is not intended for children under 16 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has provided us data, contact us and we will delete it.
